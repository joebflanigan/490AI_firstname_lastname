Artificial Intelligence in Economics | Fall 2025

Project Overview

This project examines how artificial intelligence (AI) and modern economic data tools can help analyze and transform the U.S. hardware retail sector (NAICS 444130). Using lifecycle theory and firm-level indicators, the study positions the sector as moving from maturity to early decline‚Äîespecially among independent and cooperative-affiliated retailers such as Ace Hardware and True Value. Drawing on BLS, BEA, and Census datasets, the analysis tracks trends in firm formation, profitability, employment, and digital adoption to show persistent structural pressures on small, local operators.

AI is framed as both an opportunity and a necessity. Predictive inventory models, dynamic pricing, and customer analytics can improve operational efficiency, online-to-offline conversion, and local competitiveness in a slow-growth environment. The report argues that targeted, pragmatic adoption‚Äîparticularly by independents and co-ops‚Äîcan stabilize margins, enhance visibility, and guide smarter expansion decisions, offering a practical blueprint for digital transformation in a legacy retail segment.
